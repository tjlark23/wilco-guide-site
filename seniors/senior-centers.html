Senior Centers & Community Groups for Seniors in Williamson County

Senior centers and community groups are the social backbone of Williamson County's 55+ population, offering everything from daily activities and health screenings to classes, meals, and genuine friendship. The Georgetown Senior Activity Center on Rock Street is one of the most active in the region, with a packed calendar that includes card games, art classes, fitness programs, educational speakers, and holiday celebrations. The Round Rock Senior Center on East Main Street provides a welcoming space for older adults to gather, with regular programming in health and wellness, creative arts, and technology education. Sun City Texas operates its own extensive network of clubs and interest groups, covering everything from photography and woodworking to travel and genealogy. Beyond dedicated senior centers, community groups in Cedar Park, Leander, Hutto, and Taylor provide localized social opportunities through libraries, churches, and civic organizations. The Williamson County chapter of AARP hosts regular events and advocacy meetings. For seniors who have recently moved to the area or lost a spouse, walking through the door of a senior center can feel like a big step, but the people inside have a way of making newcomers feel welcome within minutes. These centers are not about being old; they are about being connected, curious, and engaged in a community that genuinely cares.

Frequently Asked Questions

What activities are offered at Williamson County senior centers?▼
Senior centers in the county offer a wide range of activities including fitness classes, card and board games, art and craft workshops, technology education, guest speakers on health and financial topics, live music, holiday celebrations, book clubs, day trips, health screenings, and daily or weekly meal programs. The Georgetown Senior Activity Center and Round Rock Senior Center both publish monthly calendars with dozens of scheduled activities. Most programs are free or very low-cost.
Do I need to be a member to attend a senior center?▼
Most senior centers in Williamson County are open to all adults 55 or 60 and older, depending on the center. Some require a simple registration or annual membership that is typically free or costs a nominal fee. You can usually visit and participate in a few activities before signing up. Staff at the front desk are happy to show newcomers around and help you find activities that match your interests. Walk-ins are generally welcome.
Are meals available at senior centers in Williamson County?▼
Yes, several senior centers offer congregate meal programs, typically serving a hot lunch on weekdays for adults 60 and older. These meals are funded in part through the Older Americans Act and are provided on a suggested donation basis, meaning you are encouraged to contribute what you can but no one is turned away for inability to pay. The meal programs are as much about social connection as nutrition, with many participants gathering daily for conversation and companionship.
Where are the senior centers located in Williamson County?▼
The Georgetown Senior Activity Center is located on Rock Street in downtown Georgetown and is one of the most active in the region. The Round Rock Senior Center is on East Main Street in downtown Round Rock. Sun City Texas operates its own extensive community center and club facilities for residents. Smaller communities like Hutto, Taylor, and Jarrell also have community spaces that offer programming for older adults. Contact your city's parks and recreation department for the nearest location.
