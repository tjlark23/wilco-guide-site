Physical Therapy & Rehabilitation for Seniors in Williamson County

Physical therapy and rehabilitation services in Williamson County help seniors recover from surgeries, manage chronic pain, improve balance, and maintain the mobility that makes independent living possible. After a hip or knee replacement, the right therapist can mean the difference between a full recovery and a frustrating plateau. Round Rock and Georgetown both have outpatient rehabilitation centers affiliated with major hospital systems, including Baylor Scott & White and St. David's, as well as independent clinics that offer a more personalized experience. Many therapists in the area hold geriatric certifications and design treatment plans specifically for adults 55 and older, accounting for conditions like arthritis, osteoporosis, and neuropathy. Cedar Park and Leander clinics along the US-183A corridor provide convenient access for residents on the county's western side. In-home physical therapy is also available through several local agencies for seniors who have difficulty traveling to a clinic. When selecting a provider, ask about their experience with your specific condition, whether they accept your insurance, and how they measure progress. Consistent, skilled physical therapy keeps seniors active, reduces fall risk, and often eliminates the need for more invasive interventions down the road.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many physical therapy sessions does Medicare cover?▼
Medicare does not set a hard limit on the number of physical therapy visits. Coverage is based on medical necessity, meaning your therapist and physician must document that the therapy is reasonable and necessary for your condition. Your therapist will set goals and track progress, and Medicare will continue to cover sessions as long as you are making documented improvement. There is an annual spending threshold that may trigger a review, but it is not a hard cap.
Do I need a doctor's referral for physical therapy in Texas?▼
Texas allows direct access to physical therapy, meaning you can begin treatment without a physician referral. However, Medicare requires a physician's order for covered PT services. If you plan to use Medicare, ask your doctor for a referral. For other insurance plans, check your policy or call your insurer to confirm whether a referral is needed.
What should I expect at my first physical therapy appointment?▼
Your first visit is typically an evaluation lasting 45 minutes to an hour. The therapist will review your medical history, assess your strength, flexibility, balance, and range of motion, and discuss your goals. They will develop a treatment plan outlining how often you should attend, what exercises you will do, and what milestones they expect you to reach. Wear comfortable clothing and bring a list of your medications and any relevant imaging or surgical reports.
Is in-home physical therapy available in Williamson County?▼
Yes, several home health agencies in Williamson County provide in-home physical therapy for seniors who have difficulty traveling to a clinic. In-home PT is often appropriate after a hospital stay or surgery, or for individuals with significant mobility limitations. Medicare covers home-based PT when you are considered homebound, meaning leaving home requires considerable effort. Your doctor can order home health PT as part of your recovery plan.
