How to Choose In-Home Care in Williamson County

A practical guide for Williamson County families navigating in-home care options, from understanding service types to evaluating local providers.

2026-01-15·6 min read

Why In-Home Care Matters in Williamson County

Williamson County is growing fast, but one thing that has not changed is the desire most seniors have to stay in their own homes as long as possible. In-home care makes that possible by bringing trained caregivers directly to you, whether you need help a few hours a week or around the clock. With providers serving Round Rock, Georgetown, Cedar Park, and communities across the county, families have real options to explore.

The key is knowing what to look for and what questions to ask before you sign a contract. This guide walks you through the process step by step.

Understanding the Types of In-Home Care

Not all in-home care is the same, and understanding the differences will help you make the right choice.

  • Companion care covers non-medical support like meal preparation, light housekeeping, transportation to appointments, and social companionship. This is often the right starting point for seniors who are mostly independent but need a hand with daily tasks.
  • Personal care includes help with bathing, dressing, grooming, and mobility. Caregivers providing personal care typically have additional training and certification.
  • Home health care involves skilled medical services such as wound care, medication management, physical therapy, and health monitoring. These services are provided by licensed nurses or therapists and are often covered by Medicare.

Many families start with companion care and transition to more comprehensive services as needs evolve. Providers like Caring Senior Service and Home Instead in the Round Rock and Georgetown areas offer flexible plans that can scale with your situation.

What to Look for in a Provider

When evaluating in-home care agencies in Williamson County, keep these factors front and center:

  • Licensing and insurance. Texas requires home health agencies to be licensed through the Health and Human Services Commission. Ask to see current licensing and verify it is up to date.
  • Caregiver screening. Ask about background checks, drug testing, and reference verification. Reputable agencies conduct thorough screening before placing a caregiver in your home.
  • Training and supervision. Find out what initial training caregivers receive and whether the agency provides ongoing education. Ask how often a supervisor checks in.
  • Care planning. A good agency will conduct an in-home assessment before services begin and create a written care plan tailored to your needs.
  • Backup coverage. What happens when your regular caregiver is sick or on vacation? Make sure the agency has a reliable backup system.

Questions to Ask During Your Search

Before committing to a provider, schedule consultations with at least two or three agencies. Here are questions worth asking:

  1. How do you match caregivers with clients?
  2. What is your minimum number of hours per visit or per week?
  3. Do you accept Medicare, Medicaid, or long-term care insurance?
  4. Can I request a different caregiver if the fit is not right?
  5. How do you handle emergencies or after-hours concerns?
  6. Are your caregivers employees of the agency, or independent contractors?

The distinction between employees and contractors matters. Agencies that employ their caregivers directly handle payroll taxes, workers' compensation, and liability insurance, which protects you.

Costs and Payment Options

In Williamson County, in-home care typically ranges from $25 to $35 per hour for non-medical companion or personal care. Skilled home health services can run higher depending on the level of care required. Several payment options may be available:

  • Medicare covers skilled home health care when ordered by a physician and provided by a Medicare-certified agency.
  • Medicaid and Texas STAR+PLUS may cover some in-home services for qualifying individuals.
  • Long-term care insurance often includes in-home care benefits. Review your policy carefully.
  • Veterans benefits through the VA Aid and Attendance program can help cover costs for eligible veterans and surviving spouses.
  • Private pay is always an option and gives you the most flexibility in choosing services.

For help understanding your benefits, the Williamson County Area Agency on Aging can connect you with a counselor.

Local Providers Worth Considering

Williamson County is served by several well-regarded in-home care agencies. BrightStar Care offers both non-medical and skilled nursing services across Round Rock, Cedar Park, and Georgetown. Caring Senior Service has built a strong reputation for personalized companion and personal care. Home Instead is a national brand with a well-established local office that focuses on Alzheimer's and dementia care in addition to standard services.
