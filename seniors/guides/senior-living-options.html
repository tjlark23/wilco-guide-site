Senior Living Options in Williamson County

From independent living to memory care, a comprehensive look at senior housing options across Williamson County with guidance on costs, amenities, and how to evaluate communities.

2026-01-25·8 min read

Understanding Your Options

Choosing where to live as you get older is one of the most important decisions you will make, and Williamson County offers a wide range of options to fit different needs, preferences, and budgets. The key is understanding the differences between community types so you can find the right match for your current situation and plan for the future.

This guide breaks down the major categories of senior living available across Round Rock, Georgetown, Cedar Park, and other Williamson County communities.

Independent Living

Independent living communities are designed for seniors who are active and self-sufficient but want to simplify their lifestyle. These communities handle maintenance, landscaping, and often include amenities like fitness centers, pools, dining options, and social programming.

Williamson County's most prominent independent living option is Sun City Texas in Georgetown, a Del Webb community with over 9,000 homes, multiple golf courses, recreation centers, and hundreds of resident-led clubs and activities. Sun City is a destination in itself, drawing retirees from across the country.

Beyond Sun City, independent living apartments and cottages are available through communities in Round Rock and Cedar Park that offer month-to-month or lease-based arrangements with included services like housekeeping, transportation, and meal plans.

Costs for independent living in Williamson County typically range from $2,500 to $5,000 per month depending on the community, apartment size, and included services. Sun City homes are purchased rather than rented, with prices varying based on the neighborhood and floor plan.

Assisted Living

Assisted living is the right fit when you or a loved one needs help with daily activities such as bathing, dressing, medication management, or meal preparation, but does not require full-time medical care. Assisted living communities provide private or semi-private apartments with trained staff available around the clock.

In Williamson County, assisted living options include both large, well-known communities and smaller residential care homes that offer a more intimate setting. When evaluating assisted living communities, pay attention to:

  • Staff-to-resident ratios. Lower ratios generally mean more personalized attention.
  • Activity programming. Look for communities that offer daily social, physical, and cognitive activities rather than just basic care.
  • Dining quality. Meals are a big part of daily life. Visit during a meal service to see the quality and variety firsthand.
  • Transparency on costs. Ask about base rates, what is included, and what triggers additional charges as care needs increase.

Monthly costs for assisted living in the area typically range from $4,000 to $7,000 depending on the level of care required.

Memory Care

Memory care communities specialize in serving residents with Alzheimer's disease, dementia, and other cognitive conditions. These communities feature secured environments to prevent wandering, staff specifically trained in dementia care techniques, and structured daily routines designed to reduce confusion and anxiety.

When touring memory care communities in Williamson County, ask about:

  • Staff training certifications in dementia care
  • How the community handles behavioral changes
  • The physical layout and security features
  • Family communication practices and visiting policies
  • Engagement programs designed for cognitive stimulation

Memory care is typically the most expensive option, with monthly costs in Williamson County ranging from $5,500 to $8,500. Some communities offer memory care as a wing or floor within a larger assisted living campus, which can make transitions easier if needs change over time.

Continuing Care Retirement Communities (CCRCs)

CCRCs, also called life plan communities, offer multiple levels of care on a single campus. You might move in as an independent living resident and transition to assisted living or memory care as your needs evolve, all without leaving the community you know.

CCRCs typically require a significant entrance fee ranging from $100,000 to $400,000 or more, plus monthly fees. The advantage is the security of knowing care is available on-site no matter what the future holds. While Williamson County has limited CCRC options compared to larger metro areas, communities in the greater Austin region are accessible.

Aging in Place

Many Williamson County seniors choose to stay in their own homes with support from in-home care providers. Aging in place works well when your home can be modified for safety, such as adding grab bars, improving lighting, and removing tripping hazards, and when reliable care services are available in your area.

Our guide on choosing in-home care covers this option in detail, including costs, provider comparisons, and payment options.

How to Evaluate Communities

No matter which type of senior living you are considering, these steps will help you make a well-informed decision:

  1. Visit in person. Schedule tours at multiple communities. Visit at different times of day to see how staff interact with residents during meals, activities, and quieter moments.
  2. Talk to current residents and families. Ask about their experience, what they wish they had known, and whether they would choose the community again.
  3. Check state inspection reports. Texas Health and Human Services publishes inspection results for licensed senior living communities. Review these before signing anything.
  4. Understand the contract. Read the residency agreement carefully. Know what is included, what costs extra, and what the discharge policies are.
  5. Plan for the future. Choose a community that can accommodate changing needs so you or your loved one can avoid disruptive moves later.
