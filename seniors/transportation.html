Senior Transportation Services for Seniors in Williamson County

Getting where you need to go safely and reliably is essential to staying independent, and Williamson County offers several transportation options designed specifically for seniors. The Capital Area Rural Transportation System, known as CARTS, provides demand-response bus service across the county, including trips to medical appointments, grocery stores, and senior centers. Georgetown's GoGeo transit system offers fixed-route and on-demand service within the city limits at low or no cost for older adults. Capital Metro's Red Line commuter rail connects Leander to downtown Austin, providing a stress-free alternative to driving on I-35. For seniors who need door-to-door service, several private transportation companies in Round Rock and Georgetown specialize in non-emergency medical transport, with drivers trained to assist passengers who use wheelchairs, walkers, or other mobility devices. Rideshare services like Uber and Lyft are active throughout the county, and several local organizations offer free rides to medical appointments through volunteer driver programs. The Area Agency on Aging of the Capital Area can help connect seniors with subsidized transportation options based on income and need. When planning transportation, ask about advance booking requirements, eligibility criteria, and whether the service covers destinations outside Williamson County, such as Austin-area hospitals and specialists. Reliable transportation keeps seniors connected to healthcare, community, and the daily routines that make life enjoyable.

Frequently Asked Questions

What public transportation is available for seniors in Williamson County?▼
CARTS, the Capital Area Rural Transportation System, provides demand-response bus service across the county for medical appointments, shopping, and other essential trips. Georgetown's GoGeo transit system offers fixed-route and on-demand service within city limits. Capital Metro's Red Line commuter rail connects Leander to downtown Austin. Fares for seniors are typically reduced, and some services are free. CARTS rides generally need to be booked in advance, so plan ahead for appointments.
Are there medical transportation services in Williamson County?▼
Yes, several non-emergency medical transportation companies serve Williamson County, offering door-to-door service for doctor's appointments, dialysis, physical therapy, and other medical visits. Many accept Medicaid transportation benefits, and some work with Medicare Advantage plans. Drivers are trained to assist passengers with wheelchairs, walkers, and mobility devices. Some nonprofit organizations and churches also provide free medical transportation through volunteer driver programs.
Can I use Uber or Lyft in Williamson County?▼
Yes, both Uber and Lyft operate throughout Williamson County, including Round Rock, Georgetown, Cedar Park, and Leander. Both services offer accessible vehicle options in some areas. If you are not comfortable with the smartphone app, family members can request rides on your behalf, or you can call Uber directly at their phone request line. Some seniors find rideshare more convenient and flexible than scheduled transportation services, though costs can add up for frequent use.
Is there a volunteer driver program for seniors in Williamson County?▼
Several organizations coordinate volunteer driver programs for seniors in the county. The Area Agency on Aging of the Capital Area can connect you with available programs based on your location and needs. Some churches and community organizations also operate informal ride-sharing networks for their members. These programs are typically free, though some ask for a small donation to help cover fuel costs. Availability varies by location and demand, so inquire early.
