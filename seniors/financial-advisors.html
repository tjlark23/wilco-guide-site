Financial Advisors & Wealth Management for Seniors in Williamson County

Retirement should be a time to enjoy the life you have built, not to worry about whether your money will last. Financial advisors in Williamson County who specialize in retirement planning help seniors make informed decisions about income distribution, investment allocation, Social Security timing, and long-term care funding. Round Rock and Georgetown are home to both independent fiduciary advisors and advisors affiliated with national firms, giving you options depending on your preferences and the complexity of your financial picture. A fiduciary advisor is legally obligated to act in your best interest, which can provide extra confidence when making important decisions. Many advisors in the area work with clients on a fee-only basis, eliminating potential conflicts from commission-based product sales. Cedar Park and Leander advisors frequently serve clients who have recently relocated to Williamson County and need help re-evaluating their financial plan in the context of Texas's tax advantages, including no state income tax. When choosing an advisor, ask about their experience with retirees, their approach to managing market volatility, and how often they meet with clients for reviews. A good financial advisor becomes a long-term partner who helps you adapt your plan as life changes, not someone who sells you a product and disappears.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a fiduciary financial advisor and why does it matter?▼
A fiduciary financial advisor is legally required to act in your best interest at all times, recommending investments and strategies that benefit you rather than generating commissions for themselves. Not all financial advisors are fiduciaries. Asking this question upfront is one of the most important steps you can take when choosing an advisor. Fee-only advisors, who are compensated solely by client fees rather than product commissions, are always fiduciaries.
How much do financial advisors charge in Williamson County?▼
Fee structures vary. Fee-only advisors typically charge a percentage of assets under management, usually around 0.75 to 1.25 percent annually, or a flat annual fee ranging from $2,000 to $7,500 depending on the complexity of your financial situation. Some advisors offer hourly consulting at $150 to $350 per hour for specific questions rather than ongoing management. Commission-based advisors earn money from product sales, which is free to you upfront but may involve higher-cost products.
When should I start working with a financial advisor for retirement?▼
Ideally, you should consult a financial advisor several years before your planned retirement date to ensure your savings, investment allocation, and income strategy are on track. However, it is never too late to get professional advice. Many Williamson County seniors seek an advisor after retiring to help manage required minimum distributions, Social Security optimization, tax-efficient withdrawals, and long-term care planning. Even a single planning session can provide valuable clarity.
What questions should I ask a financial advisor before hiring them?▼
Key questions include: Are you a fiduciary? How are you compensated? What is your experience working with retirees? How often will we meet to review my plan? What is your investment philosophy, especially regarding risk management for retirees? Can you provide references from clients in a similar financial situation? Do you coordinate with estate planning attorneys and CPAs? Understanding these answers will help you find an advisor who is the right fit for your needs.
