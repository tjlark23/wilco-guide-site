3:T50e,Liberty Hill sits in the western Hill Country portion of Williamson County and has experienced rapid growth while maintaining its rural roots. The landscape here is noticeably different from the eastern part of the county, with rolling limestone hills, live oak trees, and wide-open ranch land that gives every drive a scenic quality. Downtown Liberty Hill along State Highway 29 has a handful of local restaurants, a feed store, and a growing number of small businesses that cater to the expanding population. The Liberty Hill Festival, held each fall, brings together the community with live music, vendors, and old-fashioned fun. For seniors, the appeal of Liberty Hill is the space and the quiet. Master-planned communities like Santa Rita Ranch and Rio Ancho offer newer homes with Hill Country views and neighborhood amenity centers with pools and fitness rooms. The South Fork San Gabriel River runs through the area, providing opportunities for fishing and nature walks. Healthcare requires a short drive to Cedar Park or Georgetown, which is the main trade-off for living in a more rural setting. Faith communities are a central part of life here, and neighbors tend to know each other by name.
