Marsha Russell
Oct 6, 2025
★★★★☆

We came here for the first time recently after Googling happy hours in Georgetown. 2020 Market was at the top of the list, and we were excited to give it a try. The atmosphere is vibrant, with big windows, lots of light, high ceilings, and very clever use of windmill fans as ceiling fans. Seating includes booths, tables, and stools at the bar — a nice mix — and we were happy with the selection of beers on tap and wine. We ordered the happy hour priced brisket nachos and guacamole and enjoyed our drinks while we waited for the food. The nachos and guacamole, however, were a letdown, prioritizing presentation over eating. Both come with 5 or 6 long strips of tortilla chip, instead of a basket of traditional ones, which makes both dishes look like big flowers. Yes, it’s an inventive presentation. But when it comes to eating, it’s weird. If sharing, you have to budget your 2 1/2 chips. There were bigger issues for the brisket nachos. I am crazy about the brisket nachos at Ivar’s in San Marcos, where every single bite of the meat on a crisp tortilla chip is smoky and delicious, so I was expecting something similar. Instead, while there was meat scattered across, it didn’t have the smoky flavor I craved. That was the main reason we ordered them, so this was a disappointment. Even more of a problem is the flower-like design, with the chips radiating out from the center. They pour runny queso and runny sour cream over the whole thing, especially in the center of the spokes, before putting some guacamole on top. This results in soggy chips and an overall mess that has to be eaten with a fork. The manager came by when we had nearly given up, asking if we needed more chips. There’s no doubt that would have helped from the beginning, when we could have treated the whole thing as a dip. But by this point, we were just done with it. The service was fine, though I wish our server had told us we could have more chips when she brought our food. Other things on the menu look good, but I’d be careful not to order something where presentation might trump substance/flavor/texture.
