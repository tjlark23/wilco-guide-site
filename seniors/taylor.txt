3:T593,Taylor is a historic railroad town on the eastern edge of Williamson County that has been getting renewed attention thanks to the massive Samsung semiconductor facility being built nearby. For seniors, Taylor offers something increasingly rare: an authentic small-town Texas downtown with real character. Main Street is lined with murals, antique stores, and locally owned restaurants, including Louie Mueller Barbecue, which has been serving brisket since 1949. The Taylor Public Library hosts regular events and provides a cozy space for reading and socializing. Murphy Park is the city's main green space, with walking paths, picnic pavilions, and a community pool. Heritage Square downtown hosts seasonal markets and live music. Healthcare is available locally through Baylor Scott & White clinics, with larger hospital facilities a short drive west in Round Rock. The Taylor Senior Citizens Center on Talbot Street is a gathering place for card games, potluck lunches, and holiday celebrations. Faith communities play a strong role here, with churches of every denomination active in outreach and fellowship. Housing in Taylor remains more affordable than much of Williamson County, and the slower pace of life appeals to those who want a break from suburban sprawl.
