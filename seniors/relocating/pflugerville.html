📍 Pflugerville, TX

Retiring to Pflugerville, TX

Diverse, affordable, and conveniently located between Round Rock and Austin.

Affordable housing with median price around $350KLake Pflugerville with 3-mile walking trailPflugerville Recreation Center with senior programsDiverse dining scene reflecting multicultural communityClose to Baylor Scott & White and Ascension Seton hospitals in Round RockGerman-Texas heritage and community festivalsConvenient location between Round Rock and Austin

Pflugerville offers retirees a welcoming, diverse community with affordable housing and a convenient location between Round Rock and Austin. With a population of approximately 72,000, Pflugerville has grown from a small farming town into a thriving suburb while retaining a strong sense of community identity rooted in its German-Texas heritage.

Housing affordability is one of Pflugerville's biggest advantages. The median home price is approximately $350K, making it one of the more budget-friendly options in the western half of Williamson County. Neighborhoods throughout the city offer single-story homes in well-maintained subdivisions, and the lower price point means retirees can often find more house for their money compared to Cedar Park or Round Rock. Falcon Pointe, Blackhawk, and Avalon are among the neighborhoods that attract older adults.

Pflugerville does not have its own hospital, but its location provides excellent access to the major hospitals in Round Rock. Baylor Scott & White Medical Center and Ascension Seton Williamson are both within a 10- to 15-minute drive. Urgent care clinics and medical offices are located throughout the city, particularly along Pecan Street and the FM 685 corridor. The proximity to Round Rock's healthcare infrastructure is a significant practical advantage.

Lake Pflugerville is the centerpiece of the city's outdoor recreation. The 180-acre lake features a 3-mile paved trail around its perimeter that is popular with walkers, joggers, and cyclists. Fishing and kayaking are available on the lake. The Pflugerville Recreation Center offers fitness equipment, group exercise classes, a gymnasium, and community programming, including activities tailored for seniors. Heritage Park and Pfluger Park provide additional green space, playgrounds, and picnic areas.

Pflugerville's cultural diversity is reflected in its dining scene, which features a wide range of cuisines from Tex-Mex and barbecue to Vietnamese, Indian, and Korean restaurants. The city hosts community events throughout the year, including Pflugerville Pfall Pfest and Deutschen Pfest, which celebrate the city's German-Texas roots.

For retirees who value affordability, diversity, and convenient access to healthcare and urban amenities without paying premium prices, Pflugerville is a practical and welcoming choice. The city's proximity to both Round Rock and Austin means that most services, entertainment, and medical care are within a short drive.

Healthcare Access

Pflugerville does not have its own hospital, but Baylor Scott & White Medical Center and Ascension Seton Williamson in Round Rock are both within 10 to 15 minutes. Urgent care clinics and medical offices are located along Pecan Street and FM 685. The proximity to Round Rock's comprehensive healthcare infrastructure provides excellent access to both routine and specialized care.

🎯

Things to Do

Lake Pflugerville offers a 3-mile paved walking trail, fishing, and kayaking. The Pflugerville Recreation Center provides fitness classes and senior programming. The city's diverse restaurant scene spans multiple cuisines. Community festivals including Deutschen Pfest and Pfall Pfest celebrate local heritage. Austin and Round Rock attractions are minutes away.

🛡️

Safety

Pflugerville maintains crime rates below the national average and benefits from a responsive police department and active community engagement in neighborhood safety.

Frequently Asked Questions About Pflugerville

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Pflugerville affordable for retirees?▼
Pflugerville is one of the more affordable cities in the western part of Williamson County, with a median home price of approximately $350K. Homes range from $250K to $480K, providing options for various budgets. The city offers good value compared to Cedar Park and Round Rock while maintaining convenient access to the same hospitals, shopping, and amenities. Property tax rates are around 2.3%, but senior homestead exemptions help reduce the burden.
What is Lake Pflugerville?▼
Lake Pflugerville is a 180-acre recreational lake with a 3-mile paved trail around its perimeter. The trail is popular with walkers, joggers, and cyclists and provides a scenic, flat route ideal for older adults. The lake allows fishing and kayaking. Adjacent park areas include picnic pavilions and green space. It is one of the most used outdoor amenities in the city.
What hospitals are near Pflugerville TX?▼
Pflugerville does not have its own hospital, but two major facilities in Round Rock are within 10 to 15 minutes: Baylor Scott & White Medical Center, which offers comprehensive services including cardiology and oncology, and Ascension Seton Williamson, which provides emergency and inpatient care. Urgent care clinics throughout Pflugerville handle routine medical needs. Austin's hospital network is about 20 minutes south.
