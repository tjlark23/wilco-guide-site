📍 Florence, TX

Retiring to Florence, TX

Peaceful countryside living at the lowest cost in the county, with Hill Country lakes nearby.

Most affordable housing in Williamson County (median $270K)Genuine rural, countryside atmosphereLarger lots and acreage availableProximity to Stillhouse Hollow Lake and Belton LakeLowest property tax rates in the county (around 2.0%)Peaceful, tight-knit communityHill Country landscape and wildlife

Florence is the most rural community in Williamson County and offers retirees something that is becoming exceedingly rare in the Austin metro region: genuine country living at a very affordable price. With a population of approximately 1,200, Florence is a small, quiet town in the northwestern part of the county where the landscape opens up into ranch land, live oak groves, and rolling Hill Country terrain.

Affordability is Florence's strongest advantage. The median home price is approximately $270K, the lowest in Williamson County. Properties often include larger lots or acreage, and the lower cost of entry means retirees can purchase a home outright or maintain very low housing costs. For those relocating from expensive metros, Florence's pricing is particularly striking. Property tax rates are around 2.0%, among the lowest in the county, and senior homestead exemptions provide additional relief.

Florence does not have its own hospital, medical clinics, or significant commercial infrastructure. St. David's Georgetown Hospital is approximately 25 to 30 minutes south, and that is the primary healthcare option for Florence residents. Baylor Scott & White in Round Rock is roughly 40 minutes away. This is the most important consideration for retirees evaluating Florence. Living here requires comfort with driving for virtually all medical care, grocery shopping, and services. The nearest H-E-B grocery store is in Georgetown.

What Florence offers in return is peace, space, and natural beauty. The town sits near the northern reaches of Williamson County, where the land transitions into the Hill Country lake region. Stillhouse Hollow Lake and Belton Lake in neighboring Bell County are within a reasonable drive, offering boating, fishing, and lakeside recreation. The countryside around Florence is popular with birdwatchers, horseback riders, and anyone who simply wants to sit on a porch and watch the sunset over open land.

Florence has a small downtown area with a few local businesses, a post office, and a community center. The Florence Volunteer Fire Department and local civic organizations form the backbone of community life. Neighbors know each other, and there is a genuine sense of mutual support that comes with small-town living.

Florence is not for everyone. It requires independence, comfort with driving, and a willingness to live without nearby urban conveniences. But for retirees who dream of a quiet, rural lifestyle, want to keep costs as low as possible, and find joy in open space and natural surroundings, Florence offers an honest, unpretentious version of the Hill Country retirement that many people romanticize but few places can actually deliver at this price point.

Healthcare Access

Florence does not have its own hospital or medical clinics. St. David's Georgetown Hospital is approximately 25 to 30 minutes south, serving as the primary healthcare option. Baylor Scott & White in Round Rock is roughly 40 minutes away. Retirees in Florence should be comfortable driving to Georgetown for all medical appointments, prescriptions, and urgent care needs.

🎯

Things to Do

Florence's recreation centers on outdoor and rural activities: enjoying the countryside, birdwatching, horseback riding, and gardening. Stillhouse Hollow Lake and Belton Lake are within driving distance for boating and fishing. Georgetown's downtown, parks, and community events are 25 to 30 minutes south. The community center hosts local gatherings and events.

🛡️

Safety

Florence is a very safe, small community where residents know each other and look out for one another. The Williamson County Sheriff's Office provides law enforcement coverage.

Frequently Asked Questions About Florence

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Florence TX a good place to retire?▼
Florence is an excellent choice for retirees who prioritize affordability, peace, and rural living above all else. With the lowest median home price in Williamson County at $270K and property tax rates around 2.0%, it is the most budget-friendly option in the area. The trade-offs are significant: no local hospital, very limited commercial services, and a 25- to 30-minute drive to Georgetown for groceries, healthcare, and most errands. Retirees who are comfortable with this lifestyle will find a genuinely peaceful, tight-knit community.
How far is Florence TX from hospitals?▼
Florence is approximately 25 to 30 minutes from St. David's Georgetown Hospital, which is the nearest full-service medical facility. Baylor Scott & White Medical Center in Round Rock is about 40 minutes away. There are no hospitals, urgent care clinics, or significant medical offices in Florence itself. Retirees with ongoing medical needs should carefully consider this distance when evaluating Florence as a retirement location.
What is Florence TX like?▼
Florence is a very small, rural town with approximately 1,200 residents in the northwestern part of Williamson County. It features open ranch land, Hill Country scenery, and a tight-knit community where neighbors know each other. There is a small downtown with a few local businesses and a community center. The pace of life is slow and quiet. It is the most rural option in the county, appealing to retirees who want country living near the Hill Country lakes at a very affordable price.
