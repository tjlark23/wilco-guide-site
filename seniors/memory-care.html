Memory Care & Dementia Services for Seniors in Williamson County

Memory care services in Williamson County provide specialized support for seniors living with Alzheimer's disease, dementia, and other cognitive conditions. Families navigating a new diagnosis often feel overwhelmed, and the good news is that this community has responded with real compassion and expertise. Georgetown and Round Rock both have dedicated memory care communities offering structured environments with 24-hour supervision, therapeutic activities, and staff trained specifically in dementia care techniques. Several facilities near Sun City cater to the area's large 55+ population and understand the unique transition families face when a spouse or parent needs a higher level of support. Beyond residential care, Williamson County also offers adult day programs that give caregivers a much-needed break while their loved one enjoys social engagement and cognitive stimulation in a safe setting. The Alzheimer's Association's Capital of Texas chapter hosts local support groups and educational workshops in Georgetown and Round Rock throughout the year. When researching memory care options, ask about staff-to-resident ratios, how the community handles behavioral changes, and whether they offer a secured outdoor area for walking. The right memory care provider treats every resident with dignity and builds a daily routine that preserves joy and connection.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between memory care and assisted living?▼
Memory care is a specialized form of assisted living designed specifically for residents with Alzheimer's disease, dementia, or other cognitive impairments. Memory care communities offer secured environments to prevent wandering, higher staff-to-resident ratios, and programming tailored to cognitive stimulation. Assisted living communities serve a broader population and may not have the specialized training or security features that memory care provides.
How much does memory care cost in Williamson County?▼
Memory care in Williamson County generally ranges from $4,500 to $7,500 per month, depending on the community, the level of care required, and the type of room. Some communities charge a base rate with additional fees for higher levels of assistance, while others offer all-inclusive pricing. It is important to ask exactly what is included in the monthly fee and what triggers additional charges.
When is it time to consider memory care for a loved one?▼
Signs that it may be time to explore memory care include wandering or getting lost in familiar places, difficulty managing medications safely, increased confusion about time and place, safety concerns like leaving the stove on, and caregiver burnout in the family. A geriatrician or neurologist can help assess the level of cognitive decline and provide guidance on appropriate care settings.
Does Medicaid cover memory care in Texas?▼
Texas Medicaid does not directly pay for memory care community room and board, but the STAR+PLUS Medicaid waiver program can cover some care services for qualifying individuals in assisted living and memory care settings. Eligibility is based on both financial criteria and the level of care needed. An elder law attorney or Medicaid planning specialist in Williamson County can help you understand your options and structure finances to qualify.
